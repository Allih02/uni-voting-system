Privacy Policy

How we collect, process, and protect your personal information

Last Updated: May 1, 2025

Privacy Overview

At University Voting System, we take your privacy seriously. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you use our platform.

We are committed to protecting your personal information and your right to privacy. If you have any questions or concerns about our policy or our practices with regard to your personal information, please contact us at privacy@universityvoter.com.

This Privacy Policy applies to all information collected through our voting platform, including web and mobile applications. By accessing or using our platform, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Privacy Policy.

Data Minimization

We collect only the information necessary to provide our voting services, adhering to the principle of data minimization.

Strong Protection

Your data is protected with industry-leading security measures, including encryption, secure access controls, and regular security audits.

Your Control

We provide tools and options that allow you to access, correct, and delete your personal information, giving you control over your data.

Detailed Privacy Policy

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Personal Information

We collect personal information that you voluntarily provide to us when you register on our platform, express interest in obtaining information about us or our services, or otherwise contact us.

The personal information we collect includes:

  • University Email Address: Used to verify your university affiliation and provide account-related communications.
  • Name: Used for account identification and verification purposes.
  • Student/Faculty ID: Used to verify your eligibility to participate in specific elections.
  • University Affiliation: Used to determine which elections you are eligible to participate in (e.g., department, class year, faculty status).
  • Authentication Credentials: Your password is stored in an encrypted format and never accessible in plain text.

1.2 Vote Information

When you participate in an election on our platform, we record:

  • Voting Record: The fact that you have voted in a specific election (to prevent multiple voting).
  • Anonymous Ballot: Your actual voting choices are recorded anonymously and cannot be linked back to your identity.

1.3 Automatically Collected Information

We automatically collect certain information when you visit, use, or navigate our platform. This information does not reveal your specific identity but may include:

  • Device Information: Such as device type, operating system, IP address, and browser type.
  • Usage Data: Information about how you use our platform, including pages visited, time spent, and actions taken.
  • Log Data: Information our servers automatically collect when you access our platform, such as IP address, time and date of your visit, browser type, and the pages you viewed.

1.4 Cookies and Similar Technologies

We use cookies and similar tracking technologies to collect and track information about your interactions with our platform. Cookies are small data files that are placed on your device when you visit a website and store information about your preferences or past actions.

We use cookies for the following purposes:

  • Essential cookies: Required for the operation of our platform, such as session management and security features.
  • Analytical cookies: Help us understand how users interact with our platform so we can improve functionality and user experience.
  • Preference cookies: Allow our platform to remember your preferences and settings.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect or receive for the following specific purposes:

2.1 Provide and Maintain Our Platform

We use your information to provide you with the University Voting System platform, process your registration, and facilitate your participation in elections. This includes verifying your eligibility, authenticating your identity, and recording your anonymous votes.

2.2 Improve and Optimize

We use your information to improve our platform's functionality, analyze usage patterns, and optimize the user experience. This includes troubleshooting issues, analyzing user behavior patterns, and implementing enhancements based on how users interact with our platform.

2.3 Communication

We use your email address to send you important notifications about:

  • Upcoming elections you are eligible to participate in
  • Confirmation of your successful vote submission
  • Account-related updates or security notifications
  • Changes to our terms, conditions, or policies

You can opt out of non-essential communications at any time by adjusting your account settings or following the "unsubscribe" instructions in our emails.

2.4 Security and Protection

We use your information to protect our platform, detect and prevent fraudulent activities, verify your identity, and ensure the security and integrity of the electoral process. This includes monitoring for suspicious activities, enforcing our terms of service, and preventing unauthorized access.

2.5 Legal Compliance

We may use your information to comply with applicable laws, regulations, and legal processes, such as responding to valid law enforcement requests, court orders, or government regulations.

2.6 Analytics and Research

We may use aggregated and anonymized data for research purposes, to generate statistics, and to improve our services. This aggregated information does not identify individual users and is used to understand trends, usage patterns, and the effectiveness of our platform.

3. Information Sharing and Disclosure

We take your privacy seriously and are committed to maintaining the confidentiality of your personal information. We will not share your personal information except in the following limited circumstances:

3.1 University Administrators

We share limited information with authorized university administrators who manage elections. This includes:

  • Participation rates (number of eligible voters who have cast ballots)
  • Aggregate demographic information (without identifying individual voters)
  • Verification of voter eligibility

University administrators never have access to individual voting choices or how specific individuals voted.

3.2 Service Providers

We may share your information with third-party vendors, service providers, contractors, or agents who perform services for us or on our behalf and require access to such information to do that work. Examples include:

  • Data storage and hosting providers
  • Authentication and security service providers
  • Analytics providers
  • IT and support service providers

These third parties are contractually obligated to use your information only for the purposes for which we disclose it to them and to provide adequate security measures to protect your information.

3.3 Legal Requirements

We may disclose your information where we are legally required to do so in order to comply with applicable law, governmental requests, judicial proceedings, court orders, or legal processes. This may include responding to public authorities seeking information for national security or law enforcement requirements.

3.4 Protection of Rights

We may disclose your information where we believe it is necessary to investigate, prevent, or take action regarding potential violations of our policies, suspected fraud, situations involving potential threats to the safety of any person, or as evidence in litigation in which we are involved.

3.5 Aggregate and De-identified Information

We may share aggregate or de-identified information about users with third parties for research, marketing, analytics, and other purposes. This information does not identify individual users and does not include your personal information.

3.6 Vote Results

We publish election results as authorized by your university administration. These results never link individual votes to voter identities, maintaining the anonymity of each voter's choices.

4. Data Security

We have implemented robust security measures designed to protect your personal information from accidental loss and unauthorized access, use, alteration, or disclosure. Our security measures include:

4.1 Technical Safeguards

  • Encryption of personal information in transit and at rest using industry-standard protocols
  • Secure authentication methods, including multi-factor authentication
  • Firewalls, intrusion detection systems, and other network security technologies
  • Regular security testing, including penetration testing and vulnerability scanning
  • Secure coding practices and regular code reviews

4.2 Organizational Safeguards

  • Access controls limiting who can access personal information on a need-to-know basis
  • Employee training on data protection and security awareness
  • Security policies and procedures for handling personal information
  • Regular security assessments and compliance audits
  • Incident response plan for addressing potential data breaches

4.3 Vote Anonymization

Our system is specifically designed to separate voter identity from voting choices. This ensures that even within our system, individual votes cannot be traced back to the voter who cast them. We use cryptographic techniques like blind signatures and secure multi-party computation to maintain this separation while still ensuring vote integrity.

4.4 Data Breach Response

In the event of a data breach that affects your personal information, we will:

  • Notify affected users promptly as required by applicable law
  • Provide clear information about what happened and what information was involved
  • Take immediate steps to mitigate potential harm and secure our systems
  • Cooperate with authorities and follow legal requirements for breach notification

While we implement these safeguards to protect your personal information, no method of transmission over the Internet or electronic storage is 100% secure. We cannot guarantee absolute security but are committed to protecting your information using commercially reasonable means.

5. Your Privacy Rights

Depending on your location, you may have certain rights regarding your personal information. These may include:

5.1 Right to Access

You have the right to request a copy of the personal information we hold about you. We will provide this information in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format.

5.2 Right to Rectification

You have the right to request that we correct any inaccurate or incomplete personal information we hold about you.

5.3 Right to Erasure

You have the right to request that we delete your personal information in certain circumstances, such as when the information is no longer necessary for the purposes for which it was collected.

5.4 Right to Restrict Processing

You have the right to request that we restrict the processing of your personal information in certain circumstances, such as when you contest the accuracy of the information.

5.5 Right to Data Portability

You have the right to request that we transfer your personal information to another service provider in certain circumstances.

5.6 Right to Object

You have the right to object to the processing of your personal information in certain circumstances, such as when we process your information for direct marketing purposes.

5.7 Right to Withdraw Consent

If we rely on your consent to process your personal information, you have the right to withdraw that consent at any time. This will not affect the lawfulness of processing based on your consent before its withdrawal.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at privacy@universityvoter.com or through your account settings. We will respond to your request within the timeframe required by applicable law. We may need to verify your identity before we can respond to your request.

Please note that some of these rights may be limited where we have compelling legitimate reasons or legal obligations to process your personal information.

6. Data Retention

We will retain your personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which we collected it, including to satisfy any legal, regulatory, accounting, or reporting requirements.

6.1 Account Information

We retain your account information for as long as your account is active or as needed to provide you with our services. If you request to delete your account, we will delete or anonymize your personal information, unless we are required to retain it for legal reasons.

6.2 Election Participation Records

Records of your participation in elections (the fact that you voted, not your specific voting choices) are retained in accordance with university policies and applicable regulations governing student elections. Typically, this is for a period of 2-5 years after the election.

6.3 Anonymous Voting Data

Anonymous voting data (tallies and results) are maintained indefinitely as part of the historical record of university elections.

6.4 Usage Data

We retain usage data for analysis purposes for up to 2 years. After this period, we aggregate the data and remove any identifying information.

To determine the appropriate retention period for personal information, we consider the amount, nature, and sensitivity of the personal information, the potential risk of harm from unauthorized use or disclosure, the purposes for which we process the information, whether we can achieve those purposes through other means, and applicable legal requirements.

7. Children's Privacy

Our platform is not intended for individuals under the age of 16, and we do not knowingly collect or solicit personal information from anyone under the age of 16. If we learn that we have collected personal information from a child under 16, we will delete that information as quickly as possible.

If you believe that we might have any information from or about a child under 16, please contact us at privacy@universityvoter.com.

8. International Data Transfers

We primarily store and process your information within the geographic region where your university is located. However, we may transfer, store, and process your information in countries other than your own for the purpose of providing our services.

Whenever we transfer your personal information to countries outside your region, we ensure that a similar degree of protection is afforded to it by implementing appropriate safeguards:

  • We use specific contracts approved by the European Commission that give personal information the same protection it has in Europe when transferred to countries outside the European Economic Area.
  • We ensure that countries to which data is transferred have been recognized as providing adequate protection by relevant data protection authorities.
  • We implement technical and organizational measures to ensure the security of your data regardless of where it is processed.

By using our platform, you consent to the transfer of your information to countries outside your country of residence, which may have different data protection rules than your country.

9. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or for other operational, legal, or regulatory reasons. When we make material changes to this Privacy Policy, we will:

  • Notify you by email before the changes take effect if we have your email address.
  • Post a notice on our platform or update the "Last Updated" date at the top of this Privacy Policy.
  • In some cases, we may provide additional notice through the platform itself.

We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically to stay informed about our information practices and your privacy rights and choices.

Your continued use of our platform following the posting of changes to this Privacy Policy will constitute your acknowledgment of the changes and your consent to follow and be bound by the updated policy.
