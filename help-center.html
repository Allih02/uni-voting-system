Account Setup

How do I create an account?

To create an account on the University Voting System:

  1. Click the "Log In" button in the top right corner of the homepage
  2. Select "Create Account" on the login page
  3. Enter your university email address (must end with your institution's domain)
  4. Check your email for a verification link and click it
  5. Complete your profile with your personal information
  6. Create a secure password
  7. Your account will be verified against university records
  8. Once verified, you can log in and participate in elections

Note: Only current students, faculty, and staff with valid university credentials can create accounts.

I didn't receive a verification email. What should I do?

If you haven't received your verification email:

  1. Check your spam/junk folder
  2. Verify you entered your email address correctly
  3. Wait 15 minutes as emails may sometimes be delayed
  4. Click "Resend Verification Email" on the registration page

If you still don't receive the email after trying these steps, please contact support at support@universityvoter.com with your full name and university ID.

How do I reset my password?

To reset your password:

  1. Go to the login page
  2. Click "Forgot Password?"
  3. Enter your university email address
  4. Check your email for a password reset link
  5. Create a new password that meets our security requirements

Important: Password reset links expire after 24 hours for security reasons.

Voting Help

How do I cast my vote?

Casting your vote is simple:

  1. Log in to your account
  2. On your dashboard, you'll see all active elections you're eligible to vote in
  3. Click "Vote Now" for the election you want to participate in
  4. Review the candidates and their profiles
  5. Select your preferred candidate(s) according to the voting method
  6. Review your choices on the confirmation screen
  7. Submit your ballot
  8. You'll receive a confirmation receipt with a unique code

Note: Different elections may use different voting methods (single choice, ranked choice, etc.). Instructions will be provided on the voting page.

Can I change my vote after submitting?

No, once you submit your ballot, your vote is final and cannot be changed. This is to maintain the integrity of the election process.

However, if you haven't clicked the final "Submit Ballot" button, you can navigate back through the voting process to modify your choices.

Tip: Take your time to research candidates and consider your choices carefully before submitting your vote.

What is ranked-choice voting and how does it work?

Ranked-choice voting (RCV) is a voting method used in some elections that allows you to rank candidates in order of preference (1st, 2nd, 3rd, etc.) rather than selecting just one candidate.

Here's how it works:

  1. All first-choice votes are counted
  2. If a candidate receives more than 50% of first-choice votes, they win
  3. If no candidate has a majority, the candidate with the fewest votes is eliminated
  4. Voters who selected the eliminated candidate have their vote transferred to their next choice
  5. This process continues until a candidate has a majority

Example:

In an election with candidates A, B, and C, you might rank them: 1) B, 2) C, 3) A. If B is eliminated, your vote would transfer to your second choice, C.

Security & Privacy

How is my vote kept anonymous?

We use a sophisticated cryptographic process to ensure your vote remains anonymous while still being verifiable:

  • When you submit your ballot, the system separates your identity from your vote choices using a technique called "blind signatures"
  • Your vote is encrypted and stored without any connection to your personal information
  • The system verifies that you are eligible to vote without tracking which specific candidates you selected
  • Your vote receipt allows you to verify your vote was counted correctly without revealing your choices to others

This approach ensures that no one—not even system administrators—can connect your identity to your specific vote choices, while still maintaining the integrity of the election.

What security measures protect the voting system?

The University Voting System employs multiple layers of security:

  • End-to-end encryption:

    All data is encrypted during transmission and storage

  • Blockchain verification:

    Votes are recorded in a tamper-evident ledger that can be independently verified

  • Multi-factor authentication:

    Sensitive administrative functions require additional verification

  • Continuous monitoring:

    Automated systems detect and block suspicious activity

  • Regular security audits:

    Independent security experts regularly review our systems

  • Open-source components:

    Key security algorithms can be reviewed by the public

For more detailed information on our security measures, please visit our Security page.

How can I verify my vote was counted correctly?

After submitting your ballot, you'll receive a unique receipt code. This code allows you to verify your vote was included in the final tally:

  1. Log in to your account after the election results are published
  2. Go to "My Voting History"
  3. Select the election you participated in
  4. Click "Verify My Vote"
  5. The system will confirm your vote was included in the count without revealing your specific choices

Additionally, you can use our public verification tool to check that the overall election results are accurate, without compromising anyone's vote privacy. /=>

Important: Keep your receipt code private. While it can't be used to determine your choices, it's an important security measure.

Troubleshooting

I'm having trouble logging in. What should I do?

If you're experiencing login issues, try these steps:

  1. Verify you're using the correct email address (your university email)
  2. Make sure you're entering your password correctly (check caps lock)
  3. Clear your browser cache and cookies
  4. Try using a different browser
  5. Use the "Forgot Password" link to reset your password

If you're still having trouble after trying these steps, contact our support team at support@universityvoter.com with details about the issue you're experiencing.

The system says I'm not eligible to vote in an election. Why?

Eligibility for elections is determined by several factors:

  • Your academic department or school (for department-specific elections)
  • Your class year or status (undergraduate, graduate, faculty, etc.)
  • Your registration date (some elections require you to be registered before a certain date)
  • Previous participation (some positions have term limits)

If you believe you should be eligible for an election but the system indicates otherwise, check that your profile information is complete and accurate. If everything is correct and you still think there's an error, contact your university's election administrator at elections@university.edu.

The page isn't loading or displays errors. How can I fix this?

If you're experiencing technical issues with the website:

  1. Refresh the page (press F5 or Ctrl+R)
  2. Clear your browser cache and cookies
  3. Check your internet connection
  4. Try using a different browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge)
  5. Disable browser extensions that might be interfering
  6. Try accessing the site from a different device

If the problem persists, please report the issue to our technical support team:

  1. Take a screenshot of any error messages
  2. Note the device and browser you're using
  3. Describe the steps that led to the error
  4. Email this information to tech@universityvoter.com
